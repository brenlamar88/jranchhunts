J Ranch/The Ranch

A family, a piece of country, and 27 seasons

J Ranch isn't a corporate operation with a booking desk. It's a working ranch run by the family that lives on it — and it hunts like it.

Our Story

Bought for cattle, kept for the wild

The Jessup family bought this country in 1997 to run cattle. The wildlife had other plans. Within a few seasons the whitetail and exotics that moved through the canyons made it plain what this land was really for.

We've spent every year since building it into a hunting property the honest way — improving habitat, managing genetics, and guiding small groups so no one ever feels like a number. Three generations now work this ranch, and you'll meet most of them before your hunt is done.

The home place, 1997

The Land

Country that holds game

Managed Louisiana ground — varied cover, food plots worked year-round, and water that keeps animals on the property in every season.

  • Canyon rims, oak flats, and cedar breaks across varied terrain
  • Six spring-fed tanks and a stocked fishing lake
  • Year-round feed program and rotational food plots
  • Elevated blinds, tower stands, and spot-and-stalk country
Canyon rim, north range

Conservation & Management

Hunting is how we keep it healthy

Every hunt on J Ranch is part of a management plan. We harvest to keep herds balanced with the range, cull for genetics, and reinvest in habitat every single season.

  • Selective harvest and cull management on the whitetail herd
  • Native browse restoration and erosion control
  • Breeding program sustaining exotic populations on the range
  • Water and supplemental feed maintained through drought
